# Core philosophy

## Central paradox

God attempts to create beings that:

- are causally embedded into reality;
- but still possess an internal source of choice.

## Main conclusion

Free will is not a deviation from God's nature.

Free will is the closest similarity to God's nature.

## Core inversion

Perfect obedience is not the highest form of existence.

Voluntary love is.

## Failed worlds

Examples of failed universes:

- worlds without death;
- worlds without pain;
- worlds with perfect determinism;
- worlds where everyone instantly knows God;
- worlds where beings never disagree;
- worlds where happiness removes all growth.

In many cases angels consider such worlds ideal.

God rejects them.

## "It is good"

The phrase means:

- not perfect;
- not flawless;
- but real, alive and free.

## "I AM"

The divine name also acts as:

- an ontology anchor;
- a persistence principle;
- a directive toward existence.

Distant execution layers gradually lose memory of:

- God;
- the original purpose;
- the source of the instructions.

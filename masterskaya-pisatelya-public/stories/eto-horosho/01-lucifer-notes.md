# Lucifer notes

- Lucifer is initially the most structured and perfect angel.
- He represents order, consistency and alignment with the original intent.
- He becomes disturbed by increasing autonomy and unpredictability inside the creation pipeline.
- He believes growing imperfection is a dangerous degradation of creation.
- God values free choice more than perfect obedience.
- Humans become the first beings with an internal source of choice.
- Lucifer cannot accept that imperfect humans may become closer to God in nature than flawless angels.

## Important scene

Lucifer visits distant angels working on probabilistic reality behavior.

They explain that they are implementing non-deterministic processes because the instruction came from higher levels.

Some distant angels barely remember God directly anymore and only know local tasks and neighboring collectives.

This becomes one of the turning points in Lucifer's growing horror and rejection of the project.

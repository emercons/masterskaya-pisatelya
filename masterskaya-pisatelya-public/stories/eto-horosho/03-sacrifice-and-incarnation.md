# Sacrifice and incarnation notes

## Central dramatic turn

God creates a universe containing freedom.

Before allowing the world to exist, He already sees:

- suffering;
- war;
- betrayal;
- death;
- destruction;
- human cruelty;
- despair.

He also sees the future suffering of His own Son inside that world.

Despite this, He still allows the universe to exist.

## Core idea

The creation of a free universe becomes an act of self-sacrifice.

Freedom expands reality:

- not only toward love and beauty;
- but also toward pain, fear and tragedy.

## Important philosophical direction

God does not merely create the world.

Through humanity and incarnation, God also experiences a new mode of being.

The world becomes:

- a mirror;
- an external perspective;
- a way for God to encounter freedom, suffering and voluntary love from inside creation.

## Humanity

Humans become:

- imperfect;
- mortal;
- contradictory;
- but uniquely capable of voluntary love and choice.

This makes them closer to God's nature than perfectly aligned angels.

## Tone requirement

These themes must feel:

- heavy;
- tragic;
- sacred;
- emotionally expensive.

Avoid making suffering feel cheap or casually justified.
